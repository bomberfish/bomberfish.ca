If you know anything about me, you'd know I'm a massive BlackBerry nerd. I have a collection of about 20 of their phones (a collection that actually scored me a pretty interesting opportunity I hope to cover later this year 👀)

I mean, it makes sense. Since I was 5 years old, I've lived in Waterloo, the birthplace of the company. Most of my friends had parents who worked there.

So when I saw that someone in China was making a mod that gutted an old BlackBerry Classic and replaced the internals with what's essentially a modern midrange Android phone, I pounced on the chance. In September of last year, I ended up buying one of the full devices (not the kit). And it finally came in the mail, about ***6 or 7*** months later.

...sorry. Bad joke, I know.

## Specs

The Q25 comes with a Mediatek Helio G99, 12GB of LPDDR4X (insert rampocalypse joke here), and 256GB of storage. It replaces the cameras with a 50MP rear shooter, and an 8MP selfie camera. It keeps the Classic's 720p square LCD, mono speaker, and headphone jack. It upgrades the old 2512mAh battery to 3000mAh, and of course, it keeps that iconic keyboard and toolbelt with full functionality.

## Should you buy this?

No. I know it's crazy that I'm saying this after glazing the hell out of this phone, but it isn't really worth it. It could be a nice project if you have a Classic that you want to upgrade, but I'd just wait for the Q27 (a fully original device by Zinwa coming out next year), or alternatively the [Clicks Communicator](https://clicks.tech/communicator) that might drop later this year. I actually got to meet some of the people behind Communicator a few months ago, and I'm pretty excited for that device, especially since they worked with the industrial designer behind the most iconic phones BlackBerry ever put out.
